Day1- ice breaker activity (Bridge making)

Our team had just met and was getting to know how each of us think and work, building the bridge was more of getting to know how to work with each other, rather than coming up with a super sturdy and strong bridge to win the mini competition

During the bridge making, we had a little bit of conflicting ideas and troubles understanding each other’s different ideas but finally, after sketching what we envision and proposed, we came up with a middle-ground solution that combines each of our individual ideas.

Second activitity: arduino

Our group have a little bit of an advantage as most of us have experience in circuitry and arduino programming for this activity.

The activity was to make and programme the LEDs in such a way that it replicates a traffic light, when a button is pushed

After several attepmts, we thought we were successful as the leds we lighting up, but we realise that it worked EVEN WITHOUT THE SWITCH being pressed.

As it turns out, we had connected out 10K ohms resistor wrongly and the LED wrongly.

The solution was to split the LED and resistor, connecting the resistor to the ground AND switch to the input pin

And our final product is a success!!!

Final activity of day 1, to deliver a message across a distance without crumpling the paper.

During our brain storm, the most probable idea was to build a paper airplane. That could cover both distance and accuracy.

We build the design based off a design during childhood

We tried going 2 designs for distance and 1 for accuracy and only manage to conquer distance

Day 2!

We were designed circuit for auto paper plane throwing.

It took awhile as it was something new for all of us.

We had 2 DC Motors, element14 mm28 and one servo motor, fs90.

We planned to make the servo do a 180 degrees sweep and DC motors to run for 5 seconds after pressing the button

We used laser cutter to make the parts for the device.

It was our first time using Autodesk for laser cutting.

There were many issues when laser cutting, the program at the laser cutter's computer could not open the file, as a result, only PDF could be read.

However, PDF strinks the drawing, as a result my group had to redraw using the laser cutter's computer as trying to enlarge the drawing, the holes we wanted to cut will not be in cut correctly.

We only finished laser cutting after an hour as we had to wait for other groups to finish.

We only had enough time left to do split work, Wen Da used hot glue to fix up the laser cut wood while Zi An did the programming for the Arduino.

When the day ended, we only finished the Tinkercad circuit design for the two motors, one switch and the laser cutted wood was assembed.

This is the working circuit for tinkercad (below)

We found out that there has to be at least some distance from the end of the piece when cutting a hole inside the piece

the piece was flimsy and will break under pressure.

We attached the motor to the wood piece

Day 3!

We had to finish the product before 12pm today! We think that we will be able to make it in time.

After putting in the motor in the wood piece, the flimsy piece broke.

We used ice cream sticks and hot glue to fix the broken piece.

The wood piece broke while trying to remove the motor.

The soldering for the motor was not good and had a short circuit, thus we had to remove the motor to resolder.

We have issues for moving the servo using button but after the lecturer helped us, we learned that we can move the servo by set amount of degrees after pressing the button.

We found out that we should use a power supply to power the circuit, we set the voltage to be 5volts and current to be 850 miliamps.

We had an issue that the motor will not spin when button is pressed while the arduino was connected to the laptop, but with a little push the motor will spin. The amps had to be higher.
